---
title: Trade State Machine
description: The different states the system can be in and their transitions
---

When you create a trade, it goes through a series of execution states that ensure that your trade will succeed even in the case of anomalies:

```
POST /trade
    |
    v
Awaiting Deposit
    |  deposit seen in mempool
    v
Awaiting Deposit Confirmation
    |  deposit confirmed on-chain
    v
Awaiting Liquidity ─────── (waits if output reserve is low)
    |  liquidity available, tx constructed
    v
Sending Transaction
    |  tx broadcast
    v
Awaiting Confirmation
    |  output tx confirmed
    v
Confirmed
    |  output tx finalized
    v
Finalized  ✓
```

There are also a few exceptional states:

| State | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `Expired` | No deposit arrived before the trade expiry window (2 hours). |
| `Send Failed` | Output transaction failed to broadcast. Retrying. |
| `Cancelling Failed Transaction` | Broadcast failed or timed out. Cancelling and reissuing. |

We gaurantee that once your input deposit is accepted, you will definitely receive your output funds. It's possible for transactions to fail or get stuck for a variety of reasons. If this happens, Feather ensures that the transaction is safely cancelled and then persistently retried until it succeeds. 

Trades expire (`ExecutionState: "Expired"`) if no deposit arrives within 2 hours of creation. An expired trade is simply abandoned. If a deposit arrives after expiry but within a grace period, Feather will still honor it.

We continuously scan for incoming payments to active deposit addresses. Depending on the chain there are sometimes delays between on-chain events and our state machine catching up. However it will always catch up to the correct state within a few minutes.
