The best trade execution prices on the market are in on-chain Defi where fee competition is very robust. If you could easily access it, you could lever up and take advantage of many trading opportunities. However, to actually take advantage of those prices as a regular user is very difficult. There are many ways to get picked off or otherwise get bad execution, some very subtle.

In response, we built Tristero's world-class trade execution engine to always get you the best possible price. Great spot trade execution is nice, but it really shines in the things that become possible on top of great spot execution. For example, viable leverage ratios for margin trading are directly correlated to how tight we can get spot spreads. The better spot execution we can get, the more powerful tools we can offer you and the more profitable trades you can get access to.

It's easy to say "we have the best prices", but much harder to actually deliver. Here's how we do it:

The best prices at any given time are not always at any single defi venue. You have to hunt around for them or use a quote aggregator. But the quotes are often just lies. Since the best quote typically gets the trade, venues overstate the quotes and then underdeliver and call it "slippage", or don't give you the upside when the price moves in your favor.

In response, Tristero runs trade simulations on your behalf: we take the transaction calldata provided by the quote, and then virtually run it on our node as if it had gone through at the end of the last block, to see what result you would have actually got. This way we correct for quote distortions and get a much more accurate estimate of what price they are really offering. Then we pick the best one and quickly submit the trade with very tight slippage tolerance of 10 bps so that there's no space to get picked off by front-runners. We can get away with much tighter slippage tolerance without getting trade failures because the simulation gives us good information on what the real execution price is going to be.

On chains where there are flash blocks and other mechanisms to get the trades to run close in time to the simulation, we use them to increase execution certainty and protect you against bots and market fluctuations. Getting rapid execution guarantees meant also building great code for high performance transaction construction, nonce-handling, latest block awareness, and all that. Effectively, we built the machinery of a Maximum Extractable Value (MEV) bot, but for your benefit.

Because our execution engine is able to act quickly, it is also able to give you access to high frequency automatic market maker quotes with tight deadlines and better prices. This speed premium is why we unfortunately can't give you the exact transaction calldata we will use for your trade, as we need to execute faster than humanly possible to get the best result on your behalf.

But this speed and automatic nature of the trade means we need a second layer of protection from price movement: between the time when you see the price and sign the permit2 swap, and when we do our high speed simulation-protected trade for you, the price you saw may have moved out from under you. So we also run a traditional slippage limit of 50 bps on the quoted price as well. We're going to get you great execution at the time of the trade, but if the market is moving against you, we also make sure the price is what you thought it was.

These mechanisms and more are the basis for Tristero giving you access to the best spot execution on the market. They were not easy to build. But now that they exist, great execution translates directly into great additional capabilities:

Margin trading is limited by spot execution quality, which becomes the dominant cost. If you have 100 bps of slip on spot, but you want to trade with 10x leverage, you are instantly down 10% on initiating the trade before the price even moves, and then another 10% on getting out. Slippage is multiplied by the leverage ratio and comes directly out of your margin. At 50x leverage, you lose your entire position before the price even moves. So cutting spot slippage in half means doubling the available leverage ratios, which means getting access to more upside.

For example, perpetual futures DEXs are often trading asymmetrically, like a negative funding rate for shorts to balance an increased demand for longs. Maybe the perps DEX offers 14% for shorts, while the interest rate for margin trading is more like 4%. If you can lever up a 10 bps spot execution by 20x, you can collect 200% risk-free interest at the cost of only 2% slippage. Great execution means higher leverage means more arbitrage profit for you.
